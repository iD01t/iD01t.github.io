Cookie Policy

How we use cookies and how you can control them.

Last updated: September 06, 2025

1. What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files placed on your device to store data that can be recalled by a web server in the domain that placed the cookie. We also use similar technologies such as local storage, pixels, and tags (collectively, “cookies”).

2. How We Use Cookies

  • Essential: to provide core site functionality such as page navigation, security, and form submissions.
  • Performance & Analytics: to understand how visitors use our site and improve content and UX.
  • Functional: to remember choices such as theme preference and language.
  • Advertising/Attribution: to measure campaigns and attribute conversions (for example, UTM-tagged links to itch.io).

4. Third-Party Cookies & Embedded Media

Our pages may include third-party embeds such as YouTube videos and itch.io widgets. These services may set cookies of their own and collect data in line with their privacy policies. You can control loading of such embeds using your browser settings and cookie preferences.

5. Consent & Preferences

On your first visit, we present a banner to obtain consent where required. You can update your choices at any time by selecting “Cookie Preferences.”

Jump to manage instructions

6. How to Manage Cookies

  • Browser Controls: adjust cookies in Chrome, Firefox, Safari, or Edge via their Settings > Privacy menus.
  • Do Not Track: we honor browser-level privacy signals where feasible.
  • Site Preferences: if a cookie preferences link is provided in the footer or banner, use it to update choices.

7. Cookie Retention

Retention varies by cookie type and provider. We aim to keep data no longer than necessary for the stated purposes.

8. Changes to this Cookie Policy

We may update this page to reflect changes to cookies used on the site. Material updates will be highlighted for clarity.

9. Contact

For cookie-related questions, use the contact page and select “Privacy.”
